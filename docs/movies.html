My Movie Blog

By Celeste Olivarez

Blah blah blah here are my top four movies

here i will include my top four movies

How the Grinch Stole Christmas

This movie will always be my all-time favorite because of how important it is in my life. I grew up watching this movie every Christmas, and even when it wasn’t Christmas. Jim Carrey’s performance continues to fascinate me with each and every watch. In recent years, I have learned how difficult it was for Carrey to sit through the Grinch makeup. It went as far as training with the CIA to learn anti-torture techniques. This movie has become part of my personality, and I will only continue to watch it as I get older.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Ron Howard

Nacho Libre

Nacho Libre is another movie that carries importance in my childhood. I grew up watching this with my brothers, and it has become a movie I quote almost daily. It’s such a hilarious watch each time and it will always make me laugh. I love Jack Black.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Jared Hess

Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind

I watched this movie recently and found myself crying within the first five minutes of the film. Although I’ve only seen it once, and under the circumstances I was facing, this movie made me feel a certain way, and I’m scared to watch it again. The emotions I felt were so intense, and it was beautiful. I watched this with Ethan, and I’m very happy I got to see it with him first. It would have been different watching it with someone else or alone. It’s another movie with Jim Carrey, so I got to say I guess I love this guy.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Michel Gondry

Mamma Mia!

This is a movie that holds a special place between me and my mom. She showed me this movie for the first time 15 years ago. I love the mother and daughter duo in this film and it reminds me of the relationship between my mother and I. She is my best friend that tries to understand me, laughs with me and gets on my nerves. Mamma Mia! will forever make me grateful for my mom. This movie has such a funny story line and the music is so fun. ABBA has become a stable roadtrip band to play.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Phyllida Lloyd

The Lord of the Rings: The Fellowship of the Ring

A movie I grew up loving and watching over and over. A connection I have with my dad and brother. Every time I watch it, I appreciate all the real practical sets and makeup used on the vast races of goblins and hobbits. The music by Howard Shore pairs beautifully with the gorgeous landscape shots all across the New Zealand countryside and mountains. A true hero journey from beginning to end.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Peter Jackson

Memories of Murder

A newer favorite of mine. Bong Joon Ho’s Memories of Murder is a dark police procedural murder case, full of dark comedy and a detective mystery. I love watching films from other countries and seeing their point of view on the life around us. Every time I watch this film, I seem to understand and catch things I never noticed upon a first watch. The mix of humor and seriousness should not work on paper, but Bong is able to easily blend the two, giving the audience a break from the bleak murders and suspense.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Bong Joon Ho

Monty Python and the Holy Grail

I was always culturally aware of Monty Python growing up, but always pre assumed their comedy was dry British humor. Not until I sat and watched this film with my brother did I realize that Monty Python shares closer humor to SpongeBob than to Saturday Night Live. I love the idea of a group of friends getting together on a shoestring budget and making the film they have always wanted to make. No rules, just play. I consider this film and its humor ahead of its time. I can see the inspiration carried into early YouTube and modern day comedies. These blokes never take themselves too seriously, you can see the film “self expose” its own budget by showing the charcuterie pretending to ride invisible horses. An easy watch anytime with the lads.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Terry Jones, Terry Gilliam

Paris, Texas

A new favorite of mine within the last two years. I have always been intimidated by Paris, Texas as a film for its longer runtime and big themes that I felt I might not be in the right place in life to appreciate. I was quickly immersed by the stunning footage of the warm desert landscape of Texas. The subtle but beautiful performance of Henry Dean Stanton will break your heart by the time credits roll. A beautifully tragic love story between an estranged father and son looking for their mother/wife. This will be a film I watch every few years to see how it affects my view and understanding of life.

Rating: 5/5
Directed by Wim Wenders
